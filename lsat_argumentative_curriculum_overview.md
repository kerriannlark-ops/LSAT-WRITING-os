## Official timing rule
- Official LSAT: **15 minutes prewriting + 35 minutes writing**
- Extended training default: **23 / 53** (training or accommodation simulation only)

## Module order
- **Orientation + Official Mechanics** — Understand what LSAT Argumentative Writing asks for and how timing modes should be labeled.
- **Prompt Deconstruction + Scope Box** — Narrow the prompt before drafting so the essay stays inside the actual evaluative task.
- **Perspective Extraction + Stakeholder Lattice** — Map the perspectives into usable argumentative material instead of summarizing them in source order.
- **Mechanism Lab** — Build the habit of explaining how a claimed benefit or harm actually happens.
- **Evidence → Inference → Why It Matters** — Push beyond summary into meaning and consequence.
- **Thesis + Standard + Introduction** — Create a thesis that clearly states the side, the standard, the narrowed scope, and the basis for comparison.
- **Counterargument + Limitation Studio** — Train fair counterargument treatment and precise narrowing instead of shallow rebuttal.
- **Paragraph Skeleton Mastery** — Make every body paragraph follow a complete argumentative chain.
- **Outline + Essay Architecture** — Turn the paragraph system into a full 4-5 paragraph essay plan.
- **Timed Performance System** — Convert the reasoning system into repeatable timed performance.

## Three-day sprint

### Day 1
Focus: Official mechanics, prompt deconstruction, perspective reading, mechanism basics, one full paragraph
- Scope box
- Thesis direction
- Perspective grid
- One complete body paragraph

### Day 2
Focus: Thesis, standard, intro, counter/limitation, paragraph skeleton, full outline
- Final thesis
- Introduction plan
- Counter paragraph plan
- Full outline

### Day 3
Focus: Timed reps, diagnosis, revision, capstone essay
- Official or extended timed rep
- Weakness diagnosis
- Targeted revision
- Best model essay

## Read-first study paths

- **Mechanism Path** — Read the mechanism explanation first, then run a causal-chain drill before writing.
- **Inference Path** — Use the inference ladder to move from evidence to meaning to consequence.
- **Counter + Limitation Path** — Read how to steelman the objection, limit it, and return to comparison before timed rewriting.
- **Thesis + Scope Path** — Use the scope box and thesis formulas together before drafting any intro.
- **Timed Performance Path** — Review the timing rules, run a rep, then diagnose the single biggest weakness category.
